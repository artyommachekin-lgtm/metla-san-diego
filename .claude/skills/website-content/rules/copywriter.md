# Metla Copywriter Guide — Single Source of Truth

> **Read this before writing ANY copy** — blog, location/service page, GBP/FB/IG caption, offer text, ad, email. It consolidates the four rule files (`brand-voice.md`, `content-accuracy.md`, `seo-rules.md`, `keyword-optimization.md`) and the live service definitions in `constants.ts` into one place.
>
> **Precedence:** On *service scope / what's included*, the live `constants.ts → SERVICES` array wins (verify there if unsure). On *voice, claims, and SEO*, this doc is the law. If you change scope or rules, update this doc so it stays the single source.
>
> _Applies to San Diego (active) and, with location swapped, Miami (dormant). Never cross-contaminate the two profiles._

## 1. Who we are (identity & audience)

- **What we are:** a versatile, high-performance cleaning partner for San Diego homeowners and property managers.
- **Archetype:** *The Professional Solver.* We don't just clean — we solve the problem you've been putting off.
- **Primary audience:** residential homeowners. Buying triggers: **trust, health, convenience.**
- **Secondary audience:** property managers & vacation-rental hosts. Buying triggers: **speed, reliability, turnover.**
- **Differentiators to lean on:** flexibility ("eco-friendly" for families / "industrial-strength" for nasty turnovers), and reliability ("we show up, we have systems, we are not a gig-app").

## 2. Tone of voice

- **Direct & active.** "We scrub the floors," not "Floors are scrubbed by us."
- **Confident but realistic.** We promise hard work and systems, not magic.
- **Warm but professional.** Friendly enough for a family, organized enough for a property developer.
- Write like a real San Diego operator talking to a neighbor. If a sentence sounds like SEO filler or an AI wrote it, cut or rewrite it.

## 2.5 House style — what good looks like (the standard)

This is the voice the owner signed off on — match it. The model below is a **GBP turnover post that landed**; the same instincts apply on every channel.

**Gold standard (approved June 2026):**
> Guests rarely leave a bad review over the big stuff. It's the hair in the sink, the crumbs on the counter, the bed that wasn't quite made.
>
> That's the whole job of a turnover — a fast, complete reset between stays, not a deep clean. Fresh linens on every bed, bathrooms sanitized and restocked, kitchen wiped down, whatever the last guest left behind cleared out, floors vacuumed and mopped last. All inside your checkout-to-check-in window.
>
> Reliable Airbnb and VRBO turnovers across San Diego. Now booking summer turnovers.

**Why it works — do these:**
1. **Open with an insight, not a description.** Lead with something the customer already feels ("guests rarely leave a bad review over the big stuff") — not "This is what a bed should look like."
2. **Concrete, specific nouns.** "Hair in the sink, crumbs on the counter, the bed that wasn't quite made" — small real details beat adjectives.
3. **Name the service honestly, inline.** "a fast, complete reset between stays, not a deep clean" sets scope and educates in one line.
4. **One flowing list, active verbs.** Inclusions as a single natural sentence, not staccato fragments.
5. **Close with positioning + a soft CTA.** "Reliable Airbnb and VRBO turnovers across San Diego. Now booking…" — no hard sell.

**Anti-patterns — these were rejected, avoid them:**
- ❌ Staccato fragment-bragging: "Hospital corners. A smooth, wrinkle-free spread. Pillows squared and standing." (reads like a checklist, not a person)
- ❌ Brochure clichés: "the first thing they photograph for the review," "first impressions matter," "hotel-ready perfection."
- ❌ Opening with the literal line "This is what X should look like…" — show the standard through a real insight instead.
- ❌ Over-promising deep-clean tasks in turnover copy (see §6).

## 3. Banned words & phrases (the Anti-AI filter)

Never use: **delve, elevate, unlock, tapestry, landscape, game-changer, revolutionize, hidden gem, look no further, in today's fast-paced/busy world, top-notch, pristine, seamless, impeccable, masterful, comprehensive, unparalleled, cutting-edge.**

Avoid generic hype adjectives generally. Concrete beats grand ("hospital-corner bedding" beats "impeccable results").

## 4. Non-negotiable claim rules (this is what gets us in trouble)

### 4a. No guarantees / no promised outcomes
Deposits and results are controlled by landlords/guests, not us — promising them is legal liability.

- ❌ "Get your deposit back," "guarantee your deposit," "we promise X results," "100% guaranteed," "will eliminate [problem]."
- ✅ "**Maximize your chances** of getting your deposit back," "**designed to meet** property-management standards," "our cleaning **helps satisfy** lease requirements."
- The **one** allowed reassurance: our re-clean policy — *"If you're not satisfied, contact us within 24 hours and we'll return to re-clean the area at no charge."* That's a service policy, not an outcome guarantee.

### 4b. Equipment (the "1099 reality")
Our cleaners are vetted independent pros; equipment varies between teams. So:

- ❌ No "HEPA filters," no "Dyson vacuums," no "hospital-grade." ✅ Say **"professional-grade"** / **"commercial-grade"** vacuums and products.
- ❌ Don't claim anti-microbial spraying as a standard step.
- ❌ Don't claim grout scrubbing / tile restoration in a *standard* description (it's a specialized add-on).
- Allergies: talk about **dust removal**, not **air purification**. ✅ "Detailed dusting protocols help reduce allergens."

### 4c. Team size — 1–2 cleaners
- ❌ Never imply 3+: no "team of 3–4," "3+ person crews," "multi-person teams," "work in teams," or any number above 2.
- ✅ "1–2 experienced cleaners," "teams of two," "we allocate the time your home needs" (bigger home = more *time*, not more *people*).

### 4d. Surface care — all-purpose standard, specialty on request
- ✅ "Safe, proven all-purpose products on every clean." Specialized **marble / hardwood / granite / travertine / stone** care is **available upon request**, not standard every visit.
- ❌ "Trained in marble care," "pH-neutral products on your hardwood" (as standard), "the right products for each surface" (as default). (Post-construction is the exception — specialized surface work IS that service.)

### 4e. Physical scope-of-work limits
- Nothing requiring a ladder higher than **2 steps.** Nothing requiring moving furniture heavier than **~25 lbs.** Don't describe copy that implies otherwise.

### 4f. Channel-specific hard rules
- **GBP posts & offers: NO phone numbers in the text** — Google auto-rejects them. Drive action with the CTA button instead.
- **Blogs & web pages: the literal phrase "house cleaning San Diego" must appear** (see §7).

## 6. Turnover copy kit (where we slipped — get this exactly right)

**A turnover is a guest-ready RESET between stays, not a deep clean.** (constants.ts VR checklist; VacationRentalTipsPage; LaJollaVacationRentalPage.)

### ✅ Safe to say in turnover copy
- Fresh linens — strip, change, beds made with **hospital corners**, pillows arranged.
- Towels replaced with fresh, folded sets; bathroom amenities **restocked** (soap, shampoo, toilet paper).
- Bathrooms **scrubbed & sanitized** (toilet in/out/base, shower, tub, sink), faucets/mirrors polished, hair out of drains.
- Kitchen surfaces disinfected; **appliance exteriors + microwave in/out**; guest food cleared from the fridge; dishwasher emptied; coffee/paper towels/sponge restocked.
- **High-touch sanitizing** — switches, handles, remotes, router.
- **Staging** — fluff cushions, straighten decor, make it match the listing photos.
- **Floors vacuumed and mopped** (finished last).
- **Damage / left-behind check** and report; reset thermostat; confirm it's locked up.
- Timing reality: **2–4 hours**, designed around the **11am checkout / 3pm+ check-in** window.

### ❌ Never claim in turnover copy (these are DEEP CLEAN, not turnover)
- Baseboards, **ceiling fans**, light **fixtures**, blinds, **window sills** ← *(these were the exact over-promises to avoid)*
- Grout scrubbing / tile restoration
- Inside oven, inside cabinets (turnover handles guest food in the fridge, not a full inside-appliance deep clean)
- Anything implying it's a "deep clean" — if a host wants buildup removed, that's a separate **deep clean** scheduled every 4–6 turnovers / monthly in high season.

### Accurate turnover hooks (match the house style in §2.5)
- "Guests rarely leave a bad review over the big stuff. It's the hair in the sink, the crumbs on the counter, the bed that wasn't quite made." *(approved — the model)*
- "Checkout's at 11, check-in's at 3, and everything in between is the job: fresh linens, sanitized baths, restocked essentials, floors last."
- "A turnover isn't a deep clean — it's a fast, complete reset so the next guest walks into the place they booked."

## 7. SEO & keyword must-dos (blogs & web pages)

**Primary keyword:** `house cleaning san diego` — must appear in the **title tag, H1, first 100 words, and meta description**, and at least once in body. Density cap: **max 3 exact-match uses per 1,500 words**; use variations otherwise.

Variations: *san diego house cleaning, house cleaning services in san diego, professional house cleaners san diego, residential cleaning san diego, maid service san diego, home cleaning san diego.*
Service-specific: deep cleaning SD / move-in(out) cleaning SD / post-construction cleaning SD / **airbnb cleaning san diego, VRBO cleaning san diego, vacation rental turnover cleaning.**

**Anti-stuffing:** never two keyword phrases in consecutive sentences; max 1 per sentence; only ~50% of H2s carry the primary keyword. If it sounds forced, rewrite.

**GEO (local authority):** name **2 specific landmarks**, an **intersection/freeway access**, and **adjacent neighborhoods**; use local vernacular (SD: marine layer, Santa Ana dust, canyon breeze).

**AI-Overview:** the **first sentence under each H2 is a direct factual answer** (e.g., "A deep clean for a 2-bed North Park home typically runs $300–$450."). Add an FAQ section.

**Structure:** H1 = "[Service] in [Neighborhood]…"; Title = "[Keyword] | [Neighborhood] | Metla Cleaning"; meta starts with a verb, ≤155 chars.

**Internal links — every page needs ≥3:** up to the city hub, sideways to a related service/blog, and a conversion link to `/booking`.

**Anti-duplicate (doorway penalty):** rotate the intro hook per page — **A) Local Pain**, **B) Lifestyle**, **C) Real Estate** — and never use the same hook on two consecutive pages. Check `data/blog-posts.ts` for topic overlap before writing; new post must carry the neighborhood name and a meta that shares ≤3 consecutive words with any existing post.

## 8. Channel notes

- **GBP post (Update):** ~1–3 short paragraphs, one clear idea, **no phone number**, add a CTA button (Learn more / Book) to the matching service or `/specials`. Lead with the visual's point. Stop before posting; the owner clicks Post.
- **GBP Offer:** title ≤ a few words, no phone number, include the code (e.g., WELCOME40) and validity; mirror the seasonal offer on the website `/specials` page.
- **Facebook:** slightly longer/warmer than GBP, 1–2 light emoji OK, end with a soft CTA + link.
- **Instagram:** hook in the first line, short lines, hashtags at the end (mix of local + service). No clickable link in caption — point to the bio link.
- **Blog/page:** full SEO treatment in §7.
